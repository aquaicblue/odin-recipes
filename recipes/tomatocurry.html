Tomato Curry

What Is It?

This recipe is my own personal spin on the classic Japanese Curry. What's unique here is the addition of tomato, which provides an extra layer of depth and sweetness. I've tried a plethora of Japanese curry recipes, and this is by far my favorite variation. It's super customizable too; feel free to add your own favorite vegetables and seasonings!

What You'll Need

  • 200g Sliced Pork
  • 200g Potato
  • 150g Onion
  • 150g Carrot
  • 300ml Chicken Broth
  • 1 Tomato
  • 2 Cubes of Vermont Curry Roux
  • 1 Tablespoon Soy Sauce
  • 1 Tablespoon Ketchup
  • 2 Teaspoons Worcestershire

Instructions

  1. Slice your vegetables to your desired sizes.
  2. Heat neutral oil to a heated pan. Add onions and saute until translucent.
  3. Add carrots and potatoes, and stir to coat with oil.
  4. Toss in tomatoes and thinly sliced pork.
  5. Add 300ml of chicken broth, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon of ketchup. The liquid should just barely cover the ingredients.
  6. Bring everything to a simmer, then cover and let cook for 20 minutes, until potatoes are tender.
  7. After 20 minutes, add curry roux cubes one at a time.
  8. Let everything simmer uncovered for another 5 minutes, allowing curry to thicken.
  9. Take off the heat, and serve over a bed of rice.
  10. Optional: Add your favorite toppings like pickles and cheese!
