Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a classic Japanese homestyle meal that comes together in under thirty minutes. It's delicious, comoforting, and easy to throw together on a busy night

Its name is derived from the Japanese wordsOya meaning parent, and ko< meaning child

Insspite of the dish's arguablely morbid name, this is a meal the whole family will love!

Ingredients

Here's everything you need to make your own delicious oyakodon at home!

  • 300 Grams Chicken Thighs
  • 3 Large Eggs
  • 1 Small Onion
  • 2 Tablespoons Soy Sauce
  • 2 Tablespoons Mirin
  • 2 Tablespoons Cooking Sake
  • 1 Tablespoon Sugar
  • 120 ml of Dashi Soup Stock

Directions

Now that you have your ingredients ready, let's start cooking!

  1. Mix your Soy Sauce, Mirin, Cooking Sake, Sugar, and Dashi in a small bowl
  2. Thinly slice your onion
  3. To a pan, add your onions and sauce mixture. Turn on the heat and let simmer for 5 minutes
  4. In the mean time, cut your chicken into bite sized pieces
  5. Once your onions are translucent, add the chicken on top
  6. Let chicken cook for about 3 minutes on each side
  7. Crack your eggs in a bowl, and gently whisk
  8. When the chicken is done cooking, drizzle egg mixture overtop
  9. Put on the lid, and kill the heat. Let egg mixture set for a few minutes, until cooked to your prefered consistency
  10. Enjoy!
